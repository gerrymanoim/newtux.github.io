Notes for How Can a Strategy Everyone Knows About Still Work?

2018-06-06

Paper Link

Any systematic strategy works for two basic reasons (though these do not have to be mutually exclusive):

  1. Risk Premium: You are receiving a proper risk premium (i.e. for stocks with higher expected risks, you get higher expected returns). You risk loss at times when that loss is most painful.
  2. Behavioral: Investors make errors, or at least do not act “rationally”.

So what happens then when a strategy becomes “known”:

  1. Under the risk premium explanation: The price of that risk may change, but should be robust to complete elimination. It could be more risky.

  2. Under the behavioral explanation: More likely to go away (investors wiser, arb, more people taking the other side).

    Sharpe Ratio = [Expected Factor Return minus Cash Return] / Factor Volatility

Things can change in the numerator or denominator.

We don’t quite know the magnitude effect on excess return as strategy becomes known, but we can say the general direction is down.

Vol is harder. During a downturn popularity/knowledge may increase vol (through trend following and then bailing, or allocation flows). So even with no change in expected return, we’d expect decrease in Sharpe. This vol increase is less important for long term holding (as long as you make it through the short term crisis).
